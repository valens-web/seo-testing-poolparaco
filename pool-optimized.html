Propane for Pool Heaters

A propane pool heater is the fastest way to bring your pool to a comfortable temperature and keep it there no matter the weather. Paraco delivers reliable pool-heater propane across New York, New Jersey, Connecticut, Massachusetts, Pennsylvania and Rhode Island, and helps you size, fuel, and service your gas pool heater so you get more swim days every season. With dependable automatic propane delivery and local service, your pool is ready whenever you are.

Why Is Propane a Smart Choice for Pool Heating?

Propane (gas) pool heaters heat water faster than electric heat pumps and work in any temperature, so you can warm your pool on demand for a weekend, an early-spring swim, or late into fall. That makes propane especially well suited to the cooler Northeast climate.

  • Fast, on-demand heat - raises water temperature in hours, not days.
  • Works in any weather - unlike heat pumps, performance doesn't drop on cool nights or in the early and late season.
  • Precise temperature control - set it and maintain a consistent, comfortable temperature.
  • Extends your swim season - comfortable swimming well beyond the peak summer weeks.
  • Reliable supply - Paraco's automatic delivery means you won't run out mid-season.

How Does a Propane Pool Heater Work?

A propane pool heater burns propane in a combustion chamber to heat a copper coil. Your pool pump circulates water past that coil, warming it quickly before returning it to the pool. Because it generates its own heat rather than pulling warmth from the air, it works regardless of the outdoor temperature.

You set your desired temperature on the heater's thermostat, and it fires to maintain it. A heater's BTU rating determines how powerful it is; a higher BTU rating heats larger pools faster.

Can You Use a Propane Pool Heater for Inground and Above-Ground Pools?

Yes, propane heaters work for both inground and above-ground pools. The main difference is sizing: larger inground pools need a higher-BTU heater and often a larger propane tank, while above-ground pools can usually be heated efficiently with a smaller unit.

Propane vs. Electric Heat Pump vs. Solar: Which Pool Heater Is Right for You?

Choose propane for fast, on-demand heat in any weather; an electric heat pump for lower running cost if you can heat slowly in warm weather; and solar for the lowest operating cost where you have ample sun and space. In the Northeast's variable climate, propane's speed and all-weather performance make it the most practical option for on-demand heating and season extension.

Most pool customers use a 250–500 gallon propane tank, depending on pool size, heater BTU rating, and how often you heat. A large inground pool with a high-BTU heater used frequently needs more capacity than a small above-ground pool. Paraco will size the right tank for your setup. Talk to our team about tank options and a sizing consultation.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is propane cheaper than electric for heating a pool?

It depends on climate and how you use the pool. Electric heat pumps can cost less per hour to run in warm weather but heat slowly and lose efficiency in cool temperatures. Propane heats fast and works in any weather, making it more practical for on-demand heating and season extension in the Northeast.

How quickly can a propane heater warm a pool?

A properly sized propane pool heater can raise water temperature much faster than an electric heat pump, often within hours rather than days, making it ideal for weekend use and early- or late-season swimming.

What size propane tank do I need for a pool heater?

Tank size depends on pool volume, heater BTU rating, and how often you heat. Many pool customers use a 250–500 gallon tank; Paraco will size it for your pool and usage.

Can I use my existing home propane tank for a pool heater?

Often yes. If you already use propane at home, Paraco can assess whether your current tank and supply line support the added demand of a pool heater, or recommend an upgrade.

Do you service above-ground and inground pool heaters?

Yes, propane heating works for both, and Paraco supports both pool types. Ask about our Pool Heater Inspection and Pool Service Contract programs to keep your heater running safely and efficiently all season long.
